Curiosities

  1. Australia is the 6 th largest country by size, a massive 2.9 million square miles.
  2. The closest American state in population is New York, which has 19.6 million people, yet Australia is more than 53 times its size.
  3. Don’t think Australia gets snow? Well it’s all relative. The area of Australia that is covered by snow in winter is larger than the area of Switzerland.
  4. Australia is home to 10 of the world’s 15 most poisonous snakes as well as a disproportionate number of venomous spiders.
  5. The box jellyfish is responsible for more deaths in Australian than snakes, sharks and salt water crocodiles.
  6. In other news, Australia has sharks, snakes and massive salt water crocodiles.
  7. Marsupials like koalas, quokka and kangaroos can be found nowhere else in the world.
  8. The only two mammals in the world that lay eggs are found in Australia, the platypus and echidna.
  9. There are over 60 different species (types) of kangaroos.
  10. 1 million wild camels roam the Australian deserts. They were originally brought over to help with railroad construction.
